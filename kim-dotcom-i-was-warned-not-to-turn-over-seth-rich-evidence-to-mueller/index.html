In short, the Russians were not behind the theft of DNC data. There was no hack, in other words.

On his website, kim.com, Dotcom stated emphatically: “I know that Seth Rich was involved in the DNC leak.”

In February 2018, he repeated his claim — Seth Rich was behind the theft of DNC data. He was disgusted by the way the party was treating Sen. Bernie Sanders who, in the early months of 2016, was gaining ground against the uninspiring and criminally investigated Hillary Clinton. Rich knew the fix was in. Dotcom said it wasn’t a hack. And the Russians were not in on it.

Others, including Pulitzer Prize-winning reporter Seymour Hersh, agreed with Dotcom’s conclusions, based on available evidence.

WikiLeaks founder Julian Assange has also said — repeatedly — that Russia was not behind the theft of data (that there was no hack). And while he has not named Rich as the person who gave his organization the stolen DNC data, he has said he’ll provide U.S. investigators with evidence of where it came from in exchange for a pardon.

No one will take him up on it.

Dotcom also attempted to come forward with information that would reveal what really happened and that Rich was a central figure. He even reached out to special counsel Robert Mueller, via his legal team, as The Gateway Pundit reported.

That was a mistake.

In a series of tweets Friday following the announcement by Deputy AG Rod Rosenstein that Mueller has indicted 12 Russian officers for hacking the DNC (without evidence to support it, according to reports), Dotcom made it clear he could not turn over the Seth Rich evidence, according to his legal advisers.

He knows Mueller’s indictments are a joke and not to be taken seriously.

“Deep State indictment against Russian DNC hackers is so light on evidence that the DOJ should place a Weight Watchers logo on the front page. #ZeroCalories Mueller knows that his PR stunt will never be tested in Court. The ‘Russian Conspirators’ will never see a US Court room,” he wrote.
