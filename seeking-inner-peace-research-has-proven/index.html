Seeking Inner Peace? Research Has Proven that WiFi Disrupts The Blood-Brain Barrier and Can Reduce Impulse Control

By B.N. Frank
2012 research also proved that WiFi exposure can also cause the blood-brain barrier to leak.  And a leaking blood-brain barrier is capable of more than wrecking your Zen Factor and reducing your impulse control.
Several years ago, Dr. Neil Cherry published research on WiFi exposure’s potential for enhancing violence and potential homicide.
WiFi is almost everywhere now.  Have you noticed how many people of all ages are struggling emotionally despite all their efforts to feel good?
Even if you don’t feel that WiFi exposure is interfering with your peaceful easy feeling or physical prowess, not everyone is going to be as fortunate.  This includes animals like your pets.

WiFi is wireless radiation.  It’s more commonly referred to as “Wireless Frequency” which sounds kind of harmless even though it’s really not.
Wireless radiation is microwave radiation – like what cooks food in a microwave oven.
In 2011, wireless radiation was classified as a Group 2B Possible Carcinogen by the World Health Organization. Many scientists think this classification is too low.
WiFi is also referred to as

  1. Cell Phone Radiation
  2. Microwave Frequency
  3. Radio Frequency (RF)
  4. Radio Frequency Radiation (RFR)

Off and on over the last decade, media sources have encouraged us to reduce our exposure to sources of wireless radiation.  This includes CBSNews.com and Dr. Oz.
Even scarier, those who are already vulnerable to behavioral, emotional, mental and physical health issues can become worse from exposure – even if WiFi didn’t originally cause their health issues.
Drugs won’t cure this.  Remember that the next time you witness someone completely raging out on social media or you hear about some horrific crime.
Most people think that wireless is completely safe because there are so many mixed messages.  I don’t know that it can get more confusing than PBS’s Sesame Street featuring segments of Elmo hanging out with his friend, “Smarty” The “Smart” Phone.  There are also “Smart” Phone toys featuring Sesame Street characters that are marketed to parents of toddlers.
There is good news.  You can choose to use wired Internet in your home.  Encourage others to do the same.  Others are already doing this but it’s important that more people get involved.
If you’re still skeptical, I encourage you to read the manuals provided with all your wireless devices.
