(ANTIMEDIA Op-ed) — The FDA is once again at war with kratom, putting thousands of lives at risk because of the corporate-driven politicization of a demonstrably safe compound that helps millions of people manage chronic pain, depression, and anxiety. On Feb. 21, the FDA put out another ominous news release calling for a halt on the sale of kratom products.

“To protect the public health, we’ll continue to affirm the risks associated with kratom, warn consumers against its use and take aggressive enforcement action against kratom-containing products,” said FDA Commissioner Scott Gottlieb.

Two weeks earlier, the FDA released a statement making the maliciously inaccurate claim that 44 deaths can be attributed to the use of kratom. But as Anti-Media has reported for months, these claims are apocryphal at best and downright lies at worst.

Journalist Ben Swann recently released a video showing evidence that nine of the people in the report had consumed kratom laced with a powerful painkiller called tramadol. Another one of the deaths was a 43-year old man with deep vein thrombosis who had a mixture of five other medications in his blood. Another man had fallen from a roof and had nine substances in his system. Also originally included in the report was a man who had died of a gunshot wound to the chest.

In fact, there is only one supposed case of someone dying purely from the use of kratom, and this story is highly suspicious as no details have been released about it.

The newest FDA propaganda also claims kratom should be labeled an opioid because, as they claim, there is evidence that “compounds contained in kratom are opioids.”

Many believe the FDA is using a recent outbreak of salmonella, which was linked to a batch of kratom and infected 28 people, to further taint the reputation of the alternative medication.

The American Kratom Association asked:

“Why should American kratom vendors voluntarily recall and ‘destroy’ all kratom supplements as the FDA suggests because of this fractional number of cases involving potential salmonella contamination?”

After all, there are 1.3 million cases of salmonella each year, meaning the kratom outbreak represents less than 0.00002 percent of these cases. Yet the FDA is singling out kratom for an across-the-board ban.

AKA Chairman of the Board, Dave Herman, responded to the FDA with a statement:

“Once again, the Food & Drug Administration is going against sound science in its war against kratom.

The AKA has called for “appropriate product regulation to ensure safety and purity standards for kratom-based supplement.”
