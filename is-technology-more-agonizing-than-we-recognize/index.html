Even more specifically, though, there’s the apparently controlled mainstream media, which has a symbiotic relationship, including financial interests, in promoting huge corporations strategies centered in an almost quid pro quo ‘deal’ of paid advertisements that keep media outlets afloat.

It’s estimated during 2017, U.S. ad expenditures would amount to 206.77 BILLION U.S. dollars, up from 183 Billion actually recorded in 2015 [1]!  What kind of staunch loyalty do such magnanimous advertising expenditures buy for corporate advertisers?

If you’re Big Pharma, your pharmaceuticals and vaccines pseudoscience and faked peer-review research are repeated as ‘fact’ ad nauseam; if you’re a chemical company like Monsanto, there’s nothing better than living with toxic chemicals and GMOs in food, water and air; while there are still more devious ads that can numb us about proscribed realities that should matter but no longer count, e.g., harmful actions that have been made into “that’s just part of life, so accept it” attitudes.

Now, can you understand why “Stop the World, I Want to Get Off” is a perfect moniker to describe how many folks apparently feel and define these topsy-turvy times when facts and truths are discouraged in news reporting; when ludicrous distortions are created and spun to make people believe hyped stories that actually are not factual; and Internet censorship which prohibits users from accessing certain websites by operating systems that don’t want you to access them.
