Activist Post has already featured articles about Fitbits causing health issues as have many other media outlets including Natural Blaze, Daily Mail UK, and more.

At the same time, Fitbits and other activity trackers are still being manufactured, marketed, and sold to the public as beneficial – even to children.  Many doctors also don’t seem to have a clue that these devices could be causing health problems either.  When I read one Ohio woman’s experience and testimony, I felt like I was watching an episode of The Twilight Zone.

Apparently, since 2014, wearers have also been reporting to Fitbit that they are being shocked by these devices.  This is sometimes leaving burn marks.  The Daily Mail UK covered this in great detail in May 2018.  One wearer was carrying his 3-year-old daughter when he was shocked which caused him to drop her.  Fitbit blames these shocks on “a build-up of static energy in the user” which has left many victims very skeptical.

In May 2018, Natural News addressed EMF exposure from wearing activity trackers:

The cons of wearable fitness trackers do not end with the possibility of getting an electric shock (though, that certainly is quite the deterrent). One of the top concerns about wearing a wireless fitness tracker is, of course, the exposure to EMF radiation. Cell phones are often the primary target of such health concerns — but really, any wireless device can pose a health risk.
