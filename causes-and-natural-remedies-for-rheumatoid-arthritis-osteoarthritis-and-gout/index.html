If you answered yes to any of the above questions, you may have the beginning signs of joint pain or inflammation, which is commonly known as arthritis.

What is Arthritis?

It is now estimated that over 60 million Americans now feel the effects of one form or another of joint pain. The word literally means inflammation of the joints. Two common forms exist that affect that largest number of people. Osteoarthritis is degenerative and happens when the cartilage that protects the ends of the bones from rubbing directly against each begins to wear thin. Bone on bone is the end result and it is very painful.

With rheumatoid arthritis (an autoimmune disorder) is more common for younger folks and occurs when the immune system mistakenly attacks the synovial membrane of the joint. The result is inflammation and the joint becomes damaged. The body then replaces this damaged tissue with scar tissue, which can fuse the joints eventually resulting in more stiffness, swelling and crippling pain. A third form of this is called gout. This occurs when uric acid crystallizes and settles into the joints and tissues. This can be extremely painful and many times pain occurs in the big toe.
