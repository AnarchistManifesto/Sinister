Where IS Spring 2015?

Written by: 0x000216

Dees Illustration

Catherine J. Frompovich
Activist Post

Springtime, when birds return with their chirping songs and Mother Earth opens up offering all sorts of flowering bulbs to enhance beautiful spring days, where—oh where—is it?

It’s now the last day of March 2015, and not very much natural spring-like activity is happening around Southeastern Pennsylvania where I live. Everything is very late this year, and spring definitely has been getting later year after year, or so it seems.

I’ve not seen any forsythia yet; it usually opened its beautiful yellow flowers sometime early in March in years past. Where are the wild marsh buttercups that would carpet entire acres of fields that abut to flowing streams with cheerful golden hues?

Where is the wild skunk cabbage that rose up majestically, seemingly overnight, down by the woodland stream? No sight of that either!

Some robins have arrived in town—a little late, too—but not many other seasonal birds, whose lovely morning ‘prayer songs’ I would awaken to, are to be seen or heard. The grass—if you can call it that—is so burned from whatever was in the horrendous ‘snowfall’ this past winter, that it made the rolling lawns in the condo where I live, look burned. In years past, lawns would be greening up by now!

In my Southeastern section of Pennsylvania, we’ve had a constant deep ‘snow’ covering since January—frozen rock hard—with almost daily increments from new fallen ‘snow’ and ice! The ice this winter was more than anyone wanted to encounter. The ‘snow’ left funny circles on my car’s windshield after a night’s ‘snowfall’. Never remembered anything like that in my 76 years!

Furthermore, the earth and fields cannot accommodate the traditional St. Patrick’s Day (March 17th) planting date for peas, green onions, and other early crops. The earth was more like cement rather than fertile, plant-life-growing soil!

In years past, the heating system would be gearing down by now; currently, it’s still running like it did in February!

The skies are leaden-gray and nasty looking—very little sunshine comes our way in Pennsylvania. We have nothing but dark, foreboding skies, and on those few days when the sun is visible for short periods, there’s those damn planes spraying chemtrails from one end of the horizon to the other, doing their weather geoengineering shtick!

Snow is forecast for the northern suburbs overnight! Home gardeners will not be able to grow food this season because of all the ‘wet-out’ that’s being programmed, I predict. Back yard food gardens either won’t get planted, or if they get planted late, will be drowned out by all the rain that will come, if this past winter’s ‘precipitation’ has been any indicator of what the weather planners have in mind for the East Coast. All this while California is dry, dry, dry!

Personally, I’m beginning to wonder about our food supply in the USA. Crops are being impacted on many levels. Citizens ought to become concerned about the future of food for many reasons: availability, nutrition content, but even more so—its toxic ingredients from all the chemtrails being sprayed and rained down upon us.

Whatever you do, please don’t allow your children to eat snow. Don’t play in the rain—it’s totally toxic. Start considering the real problems with weather geoengineering, as they are quite apparent to us on the East and West Coasts. Don’t think that anywhere in between it’s safe either—it’s not!
