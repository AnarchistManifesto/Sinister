For those who don’t know history, it’s said that often they have to repeat it to learn from it.  That may be one of the “old wives tales” from a gone-by era in this modern tech age, but there just may be something to it when one examines the current state of political events both in the USA and globally.

One of the most interesting ‘things’ dramatically driving that perspective home for me was the motion picture, big screen film Darkest Hour.  Granted, the film probably used literary license in much of its dialogue but, historically, it portrays an ‘artifact’ of history, i.e., Great Britain’s bold stand in opposing Herr Adolph Hitler and his control-crazy, autocratic, tech-crazy-human-experimentation Nazi regime.  I remind readers I lived during that time and remember what went on!

The role unpopular Prime Minister Sir Winston Churchill took in strategizing Britain’s defeating Hitler was absolutely brilliant insofar as he stayed the course and did not cave to detractors and those who later were to be identified as ‘sell outs’.   His unique style was his greatest asset!

Portrayed cinema-graphically, were politicking aspects where scheming and opposing politicians behind-the-scenes activities from dissenting factions, who even influenced the British Monarch, reminded me, at least, of shenanigans going on in the USA today because of the election of Donald J. Trump as president and the administration’s agenda about “draining the swamp” plus other social and technological issues, which many in this country feel have devolved into divisive issues.

The movie Darkest Hour not only is a theatrical depiction of one of the most important times in the history of humankind and this planet regarding defeating oppressive domination by negative and technology-oriented entities, as Hitler was involved with extraterrestrial phenomena, it somehow points to what seems to be lacking in today’s ‘gotcha’ world, with a most interesting dichotomy: those in modern government who have committed crimes are elevated to folk heroes.  Where has human nature gone wrong in its collective thinking?

  1. the Washington, DC, “crowd” we elect by our votes and whom we think run this country;
  2. the Shadow Government, which apparently includes many controlling critters, movers-and-shakers from the military and even ‘closet’ members in Congress and last, but not least, probably a segment of the one percent ‘elites’ and innovative corporate moguls; and
