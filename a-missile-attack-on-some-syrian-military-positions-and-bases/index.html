A missile attack on some Syrian military positions and bases

News

Written by: 0x000216

May 13, 2018

Sana State News Agency (SANA) reported early Monday morning (April 30th) launching a missile attack on some of its positions and bases at 10:30 pm in Hama and Aleppo provinces.

The Syrian government has called them “enemy missiles,” not referring to who shot these missiles, reports AFP.

Shortly after the attacks, the European-Mediterranean seismological center said the explosions caused an earthquake of 2.6 in the area.

According to a Syrian source, two ammunition depots on the outskirts of Aleppo and Hama were targeted by unexploded missiles, resulting in massive explosions and extensive fire. He also said the targeted ammunition depot on the eastern Aleppo suburb is located between Al-Nairb and Al Maleci, and the news about the targeting of the Aleppo airport is inaccurate.

According to the Syrian official news agency, this rape, at the same time as the attempts to reach agreement on the withdrawal of terrorists from the Yalda, Bebila, Beit Sahm and Alirukou camps, and after the heavy defeat of the terrorists against the Syrian army in the suburbs, Damascus and their departure from the eastern hinterland and the eastern claman.

Tshrin newspaper reported on its Facebook page that nine missiles were fired at the attacks.

Meanwhile, the Skyline television network, according to Syrian activists, claimed that more than 40 people lost their lives during the Sunday missile attacks on “Syrian Army and Iranian-backed forces” and about 60 people were injured.

The Iranian news agency Tsensim denied the deaths of Iranian forces in Aleppo in response to these reports, according to the commander of the Fatimid group.

Israeli newspaper Haaretz writes in the same vein that “some reports claim Israel is responsible for the attack (Sunday night).”

Earlier on Sunday, Israeli Defense Minister Avigdor Lieberman said the country would protect “free-riding operations” in Syria.

Analysts believe that Western-Arab media with fake statistics on human and inhumane casualties last night’s attacks on Syria seek to change the clashes from the Syrian-Israeli to the Iranian-Israeli.

Sputnik has recently reported that former Israeli Prime Minister Ehud Olmert, who ordered an attack on Syria in 2007 to claim the destruction of a nuclear reactor in the Deir-Salur area, has acknowledged the country’s inability to take similar action on Iran.

Ehud Olmert said on Sunday at the annual Jerusalem Post newspaper in New York, referring to the 2007 invasion of Syria, saying what we could do with Syria can not be done with Iran.
