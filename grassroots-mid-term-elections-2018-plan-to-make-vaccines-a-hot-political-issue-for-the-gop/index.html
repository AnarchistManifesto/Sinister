It’s coming up on mid-term elections in November 2018 and some individuals are getting antsy about President Donald J. Trump’s apparent failure to address an issue he brought to the forefront while campaigning in 2016: Vaccines Cause Autism!

Then-presidential-candidate Trump was joined by another 2016 presidential contender Dr. Ben Carson, MD, who as a physician reiterated his position on vaccines given to infants and children.  To date neither has revisited their respective remarks and what voters apparently took as a platform issue to be addressed by Mr. Trump if he were to become president.

Mr. Trump is now the 45th president of the United States and a grassroots movement is being organized to make vaccine issues, especially Autism, a 2018 mid-term election issue for all candidates, especially GOP candidates and contenders.

What few voters probably fail to realize is, it was the GOP Republican President Ronald Reagan who committed the greatest healthcare faux pas affecting individual freedom of choice in healthcare when he signed into law

The National Childhood Vaccine Injury Act (NCVIA) of 1986 (42 U.S.C. §§ 300aa-1 to 300aa-34) was signed into law by President Ronald Reagan as part of a larger health bill on Nov 14, 1986, in the United States, to reduce the potential financial liability of vaccine makers due to vaccine injury claims. [1]

The Republican Party did the damage, so the Republican Party should be made liable for correcting it, in my and other’s opinion, as Dr. Bart Classen, MD, proposes in his July 3, 2018 press release [2].

In part of the Classen press release, Doctor states:

While president elect Trump discussed creating a Vaccine Safety Committee whose purpose would be to evaluate each and every vaccine for its safety, efficacy and cost effectiveness, this has not manifested. In the face of more than a decade of parental outrage citing harm to their children, notwithstanding the President’s adamant position that Vaccines are linked to Autism, Trump appointees running the FDA, CDC, and HHS continue to implement dangerous vaccine policies resulting in States mandating immunization. None of these vaccines have not been adequately tested for safety.

For example, the recombinant hepatitis B vaccines, which is imposed on every American infant on the day or even hour of birth received FDA approval based on safety studies that followed the subjects for only about 7 days post immunization. Need we say more? [CJF emphasis]

Unfortunately for the GOP, Parents and other vaccine safety advocates, disillusioned by the GOP’s inaction, are being encouraged to withhold support for the GOP in the current local and State campaigns. If GOP wants the support of the millions of organized voting families that supported Trump’s election due to his position on vaccines, the administration would be wise to take, at this critical midterm elections, some concrete action to address the vaccine safety crisis as well as to inform GOP candidates of the President’s position on vaccines.  State-to-State, organized coalitions are planning to ask GOP candidates about their position on vaccine mandates. It would be advantageous, if not intelligent, that GOP candidates be familiar and aligned with the President’s intention, though not yet manifested, to evaluate the safety of vaccines and impress on the president the need to keep his campaign promise regarding vaccines.

Nothing explains more succinctly the need to hold the GOP’s feet to the fire for correcting the vaccine damage fiasco, which resulted from the GOP’s President Ronald Reagan’s highly lobbied action, than the following video wherein Bernadine Healy, MD, former NIH director, explains what everyone in medicine should know but no one in the CDC, FDA or Congress will address: biological individuality that makes each child unknowingly susceptible to vaccines toxic ingredients especially, “to a component of vaccines like mercury,” as Dr. Healy points out.
