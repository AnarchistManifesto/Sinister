How Al-Saud attacks human rights

News

Written by: 0x000216

May 19, 2018

 In recent days, a number of academics, human rights activists and attorneys of political prisoners have been arrested by Saudi security forces.

In addition to the Saudi security organization, it is said that new organizations, including the cyber-security organization, have been involved in these detentions by Saud al-Qahtani, media advisor to Saudi prime minister Mohammed bin Salman.

Social network activists have also emphasized that Saudi officials on Thursday found numerous new figures, including men and women active in the field of human rights and opposition to the normalization of relations with Israel in Saudi Arabia, such as Dr. Mohammed al-Rubaye, Dr. Ebrahim al-Madimiygan, and women activists such as Ladin Al-Hazlul, Aziza Elyusov And Iman al-Nafj?n, as well as Nour-e faqih, have been arrested by the sister of the imprisoned Mansour jurisprudent.

In the same vein, Saudi Arabian activist Abdullah al-Ghamdi stated: “Saudi officials have still kept mothers and my brothers for more than 50 days and not allowing my family to visit them.”

Al-Ghademi said last March that the Saudi security service arrested her mother, who was over 60, and her younger brother in Jeddah with the intent of threatening and under pressure.

Earlier, Amnesty International approved last year’s imprisonment of two human rights activists for 14 years and another seven years for charges of forming an unlicensed population in Saudi Arabia.

Saudi authorities have since imprisoned a large number of opponents of the regime since September of the previous year in a campaign that was more like kidnapping, and more than six months later, the number of detainees and the charges against them have not been officially announced.

Reports from inside Saudi Arabia and information provided by the families of these individuals to the media indicate that the detainees were imprisoned without a warrant or a judicial summons. They were barred from communicating with their families or having a lawyer after the arrest.

Saudi news agencies have arrested seven activists supporting women’s rights in another news agency, Agence France-Presse.

The report adds that the prisoners of law, Aziza Al-Yusuf, and Iman Najafi are among the detainees. Earlier, they have publicly criticized the ban on women’s driving, as well as the women’s mandate in Saudi Arabia.

These arrests of prominent women’s rights activists in Saudi Arabia occurred a few weeks before the lifting of the ban on driving for women.

The reason for these arrests is not clear, but activists say the Saudi authorities are trying to silence the women.

A state-run television channel in Saudi Arabia reported that the women were arrested for their contacts with a foreign country.

Mohammed bin Salman has ordered the arrest of religious figures, academics, and social, political and human rights activists several times since he was appointed to the Crown Prince of Saudi Arabia in September (September-October).

In November 2017, he detained a number of princes and officials and merchants of Saudi Arabia for corruption and released them after settling financial accounts with them.
