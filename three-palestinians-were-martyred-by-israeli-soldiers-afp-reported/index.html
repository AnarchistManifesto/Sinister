Protesters in the Gaza Strip have organized demonstrations in the weeks leading up to the anniversary of the formation of a fake country of Israel, called “return”, and in order to defend the right of returnees to return to their “ancestral” territories.

This move continues for six weeks from “Earth Day” to “Misery Day”. A return march with the aim of protecting the right to return to Palestinians displaced from their lands or escaped during the forties that led to the formation of a fake country of Israel.

More than 1,500 Palestinians have been injured since the start of this protest movement, and no Israelis have been killed during the protests.

On the other hand, according to local sources in Judea and Samaria, Jewish settlers stormed the village of Ramsey of Ramallah and damaged dozens of Palestinian vehicles.

According to the report, Jewish settlers also wrote racist slogans on Palestinian homes.

The raids of the Jewish settlers on Palestinians, in particular the radical Jewish group known as the “Teddy Althman” have increased in the West Bank and damage to their property in recent months.

Lebanon’s Al-Awdah website also reported that Zionist of the West Bank launched a massive assault on the West Bank of the Jordan River and arrested a number of Palestinians, including Professor Mardar, the Director General for Legal follow-up of the Resistance Committee to Build the Wall and Settlements.

In this regard, US Secretary of State Mike Pampeo, who traveled to Jordan, at a joint press briefing with his Jordanian counterpart, Ayman al-Sefidi, in Amman, said: “America supports a two-state solution to the Palestinian-Israeli problem. I support the decision of Tramp to recognize the Jerusalem as the capital of Israel. We call the Palestinians to the negotiating table, because it is important to achieve peace in the Middle East.

US President Donald Tramp has recently accused Palestinians of not being prepared for talks and do not know the value of US aid. He threatened to cut off US funding from Palestine in the absence of Palestinians at the talks.

Relations between the United States and the Palestinians have deteriorated since December last year as Trump called Jerusalem as the capital of the Israeli regime.

On the other hand, Sputnik has reported that Saudi Arabia’s crown prince, Mohammad Ben Salman, has criticized Palestinian President Mahmoud Abbas at a meeting held on March 27 with leaders of American Jewish organizations in New York.

In this regard, Ben Salman added that Palestinian leaders have lost opportunities one by one over the past forty years and rejected all the proposals.
