FBI Raids L.A. Department of Water and Power — Did it Miss the Real Crime?

World

Written by: 0x000216

August 27, 2019

By Janet Phelan

The FBI has declined to answer questions as to whether or not its raid on the Los Angeles Department of Water and Power today had anything to do with allegations that the LADWP has covertly set up a double line water system throughout the city, with disturbing implications.

LA FBI Press spokesman Katherine Gullotta told this reporter that she was unable to comment on the reason for the raid. The Los Angeles Times had reported that the raid was due to a billing scandal, which Gullotta also declined to confirm.

“At this time no charges have been brought,” she stated. She went on to say that “Due to the fact that this is an ongoing investigation, no further comment can be made at this time.”

Back when this reporter was writing a weekly column for the Santa Monica Daily Press, a request to Santa Monica City Hall for water records resulted in her termination from the SMDP. Further requests to the LADWP resulted in that office creating entirely new blueprints to fulfill the Public Records Act request, each of which were stamped “redacted.” “National security” was cited as the reason for the redactions. However, subsequent contacts with city crews working on water lines produced blueprints which clearly showed the double main line system, which was entirely absent from the redacted prints.

The double line system, which also features remote control valves, enables the injection of a chemical or biological agent into preselected service lines, serving preselected dwellings. The city has remained silent on this capability and the implications for its facilitating a  potential pandemic-like attack, selectively targeting individuals and their families.

The LADWP press office also declined to comment on the warrant or to verify the story in the LA Times on the billing issue.
