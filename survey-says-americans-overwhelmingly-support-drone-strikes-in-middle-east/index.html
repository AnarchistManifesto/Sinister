Survey Says Americans Overwhelmingly Support Drone Strikes in Middle East

News

Written by: 0x000216

May 19, 2018

Op-Ed by Cassius Methyl
June 1, 2015

(ANTIMEDIA) The American public overwhelmingly supports US drone strikes, according to a new poll from PEW. Out of those who took the survey, 58% of people support drone strikes, while only 35% disapprove.

According to the PEW poll:

“The national survey by the Pew Research Center, conducted May 12-18 among 2,002 adults, finds that 58% approve of the U.S. conducting missile strikes from drones to target extremists in such countries as Pakistan, Yemen and Somalia. About a third (35%) disapprove of U.S. drone attacks.

Public opinion about U.S. drone strikes has changed only modestly since February 2013, when 56% approved and 26% disapproved of drone attacks. Support for drone strikes crosses party lines, though Republicans (74%) are more likely than independents (56%) or Democrats (52%) to favor the use of drones to target extremists.”

So what does this mean? For the perspective of an activist, this means we haven’t done enough to educate our people about what drone strikes actually are. As The Guardian notes, although only 41 men were targeted in US drone strikes in Pakistan and Yemen, 1,147 people died in the strikes as of November 2014.

Drone strikes are aerial PTSD machines that kill large numbers of innocent people, while the alleged militants targeted are rarely killed without collateral damage and are kept mostly secret from the American public. Drone warfare is partially responsible for the staggering rate of PTSD symptoms in Yemen, with a majority of children said to be suffering from psychological trauma brought on by drone strikes.

These drones are death machines that serve a non-transparent agenda that is arguably more damaging to national security than the benefits claimed by military officials and the Obama administration. These drones kill 13 year old innocent Yemini boys who say they ‘dream of drones,’ creating future generations of people outraged at US drone imperialism. The US Drone policy is literally an incubation program that creates a never ending cycle of hate-fodder for terrorists-in-the-making. The media never talks about these victims.

Last week, 4 people were killed by drone strikes in Pakistan. However, statisticians and politicians don’t specify whether the “suspected militants” killed in drone strikes are actual terrorists or just happen to be innocent males within the “fighting age.” Anyone killed in a drone strike within the US-defined “fighting age” is automatically considered a terrorist — innocent or not.

If you consider it your civic duty to oppose and do what you can to end injustice and tyranny, you’ve got work to do when it comes to stopping the cowardly drone war. A good place to start informing people of this horrendous policy is to share this article.
