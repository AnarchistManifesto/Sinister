Like Alcohol, Exposure to WiFi Kills Brain Cells

World

Written by: 0x000216

September 9, 2019

By B.N. Frank

2012 research confirmed that exposure to all sources of  wireless radiation – mostly commonly referred to as WiFi  – can disrupt the blood-brain barrier which can cause it to leak.  One doctor provided medical testimony in a short video about how exposure can also kill brain cells.

﻿

If you grew up in the ’80s, you may find yourself thinking of the “this is your brain – this is your brain on drugs” commercials with the egg being dropped in the super-hot frying pan.  Earlier this year, the ad was parodied on The Late Late Show with James Corden after President Trump made a statement about how he would like to see the U.S. “spend a lot of money on “great commercials showing how bad (drug use) is.” 

You may not notice exposure to WiFi affecting your brain or the rest of you.  However, exposure may still be harming the ones you love – including your pets.  Harm from exposure is cumulative which means no one is actually immune to it.  Of course,

  1. Not everyone will be affected in the same way or to the same degree
  2. It’s not just about cancer risk
  3. Some will tolerate exposure better than others

Of course, this can be said of exposure to ALL toxins – not just sources of toxic WiFi.

Despite all of this – there are so many sources of WiFi already and unfortunately more seem to be on their way due to highly promoted campaigns for the “Race for 5G,” The Internet of Things (IoT), “Smart Cities,” and utility “Smart” Meters.  Some sources of WiFi are even being prescribed or endorsed by the medical community – even though insurance companies aren’t covering wireless radiation exposure risk anymore.  How’s that for confusing and hypocritical.

So wouldn’t it be awesome if a lot of money was also spent on great commercials showing how bad WiFi exposure is so we could reduce its harmful effects on the public?
