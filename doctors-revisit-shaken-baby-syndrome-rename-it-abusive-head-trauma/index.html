Shaken Baby Syndrome (SBS), a topic I have written about numerous times, including with Harold E Buttram, MD, is being revisited by a medical profession having some doubts and, consequently, it’s been renamed “abusive head trauma.”

Also, the mainstream media, e.g., The New York Times, has written an article about medicine’s Johnny-come-lately questioning, which appeared online September 13, 2015, in Clyde Haberman’s report “Shaken Baby Syndrome: A Diagnosis That Divides the Medical World.” [1]

As Haberman says, “It is defined by a constellation of symptoms known as the triad: brain swelling, bleeding on the surface of the brain and bleeding behind the eyes,” which also can be caused by toxic chemicals to the brain! But, where and how would a baby get chemicals? More easily and legally than anyone would guess: the baby’s pediatrician/MD administering vaccinations starting 24 hours after birth (the Hepatitis B vaccine) and at 2, 4 and 6-month ‘well’ baby visits! A redeeming aspect in Haberman’s article, for me at least, is this:

For years, those three symptoms by themselves were uniformly accepted as evidence that a crime had been committed, in even the absence of bruises, broken bones or other signs of abuse. While many doctors, maybe most, still swear by the diagnosis, a growing number have lost faith. [1] [CJF emphasis added]

What I’ve underscored above, I believe, is crucial to establishing if physical abuse, especially the shaken baby type, has occurred—something I’ve written about before. For a brain to swell, bleed on the surface and have bleeding behind the eyes in an infant, a child wouldn’t have to be shaken extremely hard or have its head banged against some hard object like a wall. Infants’ necks are notoriously ‘wobbly’ and everyone who is handed an infant to hold is cautioned about holding the neck/head correctly so it doesn’t ‘flop’ and cause damage, i.e., a possible broken neck. That, alone, in infants is basic anatomy and physiology! However, expert witness MDs who testify to SBS without physical damage evidence probably even could challenge the laws of gravity!

Because of the medical profession’s dogmatic and skewed insistence regarding SBS diagnoses, parents and others—child caregivers, baby sitters—have been charged with, prosecuted and imprisoned for killing infants in their care while maintaining their innocence. However, one MD, who testified at an SBS trial, has come to the realization that he may have been wrong and is going about studying the mechanics of SBS to delineate and clarify it medically and probably legally.

That MD is Dr Patrick Barnes, a neuroradiologist, formerly at Children’s Hospital in Boston, now at Stanford University. During the SBS trial for European nanny Louise Woodward, held in Boston, Dr Barnes was adamant about his testimony that Woodward had shaken 8-month-old Matthew Eapen. So what changed the doctor’s mind?
