5G Running Hot in Cleveland Thanks to T-Mobile Acknowledging Potential Liability in 2014

World

Written by: 0x000216

August 27, 2019

By B.N. Frank

Last February, telecom executives gave congressional testimony that they could NOT provide any studies that prove that 5G technology is safe.

Unfortunately, plenty of research already proves it isn’t (see 1, 2, 3).  One of the side effects from exposure is excessive sweating (Yuck).  Regardless, on Friday, T-Mobile unleashed 5G in parts of Cleveland which is already hot enough during the summer.

Of course, T-Mobile isn’t the only one forcing unsafe 5G on Americans – many of whom aren’t signing up for it anyway.  That doesn’t matter though because taxpayers are essentially paying for 5G – whether they want it or not. 

Verizon (see 1, 2) and AT&T already have installed 5G in the U.S. and are installing more.  Sprint has plans to install it as well (see 1, 2).  This is despite the fact that all of them have also been warning investors that they may eventually be held liable for causing harm with their products and infrastructure (i.e. cell towers).

An increasing number of U.S. elected officials are trying to stop 5G; however, some are still hell-bent on installing it everywhere despite additional warnings from

  1. Cybersecurity experts, (see 1, 2)
  2. Environmentalists (see 1, 2, 3)
  3. Meteorologists, NASA, NOAA, the U.S. Navy
  4. Utility companies

who insist that installing 5G everywhere could lead to widespread catastrophic events.  The Federal Communications Commission (FCC) is blowing off these warnings too.

Worldwide opposition to 5G is growing.  Brussels and Vaud, Switzerland have declared moratoriums on installation.  U.S. opposition to 5G is growing, too, but unfortunately it seems it’s going to take more Americans taking action to stop the insanity here.
