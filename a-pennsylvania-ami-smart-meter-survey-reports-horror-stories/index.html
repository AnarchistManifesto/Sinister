Godshall kept “opt-out” from AMI SMs bills from being brought to the floor for a vote for almost 8 years!  Maybe a total of 24 bills?  Godshall deprived Pennsylvanians of their legal rights and, therefore, should be prosecuted for that, in many Pennsylvanians’ opinions!

The questions all PA voters should be asking this November election cycle are, “How come you didn’t stand up to Godshall and demand U.S. and State Constitutional rights of Pennsylvanians were protected and enforced?  Do you really deserve my vote?  Other states provided smart meter exemptions!”

Numerous utility customers—probably in the hundreds—have filed formal complaints with the PA Public Utility Commission, only to be told by the PA PUC’s kangaroo Administrative Court, the law is the law and you must get an AMI SM or have your service terminated!

However, that law as placed in the administrative agency (PA PUC) regulations was not what was passed by the state legislature, the only legal authority to pass laws in Pennsylvania.  When the PA PUC wrote that agency’s rules and regulations for utility companies, PUC based their regs upon what it claimed (incorrectly) the legislature passed, rather than what was voted upon.

There is absolute proof for the above statement, which is found in the PA Senate and House Journals of the days when HB2200 was discussed and argued with numerous legislators stating “smart meters were not to be mandatory.”  Therefore, HB2200, aka Act 129 (2008), was an opt-in bill and the language stated so.  See HB2200 §2807(f)7(2).

The other definitive proof comes in the form of the last paragraph of the March 23, 2017 letter from Dave Hixson, PA Public Utility Commission, Office of Communication in Harrisburg, PA, addressed to Mr. Thomas A. McCarey wherein Hixson admits the PUC’s obvious deliberate mistake:

As I stated in my earlier email correspondence with you, the Commission believes that it was the intent of the General Assembly to require all covered electric companies to deploy smart meters system-wide.

Believes?  The language could not be any clearer!

Do you need any more proof than the above?  And still, no one has been fired nor prosecuted for such malfeasance of public duty and trust.  Why?

Numerous utility customers, and/or members of their families, have experienced dramatic health anomalies and issues since the retrofitting of non-ionizing radiation-emitting microwave energies that AMI SMs send into their homes, offices and places of business.  Those folks are not an anomaly, as the World Health Organization (WHO) has recognized that health problem; it’s called “electromagnetic hypersensitivity” (EHS) for which the medical term is “idiopathic environmental intolerance” (IEI).

It is now 2018.  With all the microwave devices deployed besides AMI SMs, can you imagine how many more humans are affected negatively?

As a result of the above, several Pennsylvanians decided to survey utility customers about their health experiences after having had one or more AMI SMs, in some cases—I know of one person who has 3: electric, natural gas and a power outage generator—retrofitted onto “their” home, in order to determine what, if any, health reactions were occurring within the Pennsylvania population.

I was contacted during the initial strategy talks for determining the survey questions, and also vetted the final survey format before it was sent via email to utility consumers.  Accordingly, the Petition had a Preamble which stated:

There is a growing body of evidence that exposure to radiation from wireless “smart meters” is putting peoples’ health at risk. Those people who believe that their health has been adversely affected by “smart meter radiation” have so far been unable to obtain relief from either the Pennsylvania state legislature or the PA Public Utility Commission. It is our hope that you will fill the survey, as it will help us the take political action on the state level to offer protection to consumers and relief to those who have suffered health losses.  The public deserves safe electric meters, rather than state-mandated smart meters and the radiation they emit.

Here are some of the survey stats that were verified by an actuary:

  • 62% of those who answered the survey said they took steps to mitigate the adverse effects they were experiencing from the AMI SM retrofitted on to their utility service

The analysis of adverse health conditions, when numerically tabulated for results, found interesting data:

  • First place had a tie: Concentration and Memory/Learning
  • Second place had a four-way tie: Insomnia, Stress Anxiety, Depression and Irritability
  • Third place: Fatigue
  • Fourth place had another four-way tie: Headaches, Tinnitus/ear ringing, Cardiac symptoms, Arrhythmia
  • Cancer of the blood was reported by one person
  • Other symptoms were reported by over 45% of people
  • However, only 7% of respondents said they had NO symptoms

The survey underwriters, during tabulation of results, noted several observations they passed along for inclusion in this report.

  • One of the things that stands out from the survey is the proportion of people who have taken steps to minimize the impact of meters. 62% of respondents answered yes to this question. Protection took the form of placing guards around the meter including aluminum coverings, steel coverings, aluminum plates, faraday cages and faraday cages in the house. One person put up a grounding mat on the wall. A couple of people said they moved the meter further from the house. One person paid $5,000 to move a meter from the house and put up a shield.
  • Other people mentioned their resistance to smart meter installation as part of minimizing the impact. One person tried to get their city to outlaw them.
