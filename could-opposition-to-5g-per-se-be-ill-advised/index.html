Could Opposition To 5G (per se) Be Ill-Advised?

Op-Ed by Peter Tocci

From late 2003 to March 2005, I was volunteer webmaster for the Yurko Project. It was an Internet campaign to free from prison a man falsely convicted of Shaken Baby Syndrome. The baby’s symptoms did not arise from shaking, but from the administration to a sick, premature baby of a cocktail of vaccines, the primary offender in which was ‘DTaP’ – Diphtheria, Tetanus, (acellular) Pertussis. I was a practicing massage therapist and wellness consultant at the time.

Quite a large community of medical and health professionals, vaccine activists and lay persons rallied ‘round to lend support, and this put me in touch with websites and activities of the ‘antiVAX’ community. At one point, a vigorous campaign against vaccination arose based on the poisonous preservative used in most vaccines, especially in multi-dose vials, namely Thimerosal, or ethyl mercury.

Being an avid student of the history of medicine and of vaccines especially, I had learned that there were a number of threats vaccination posed, including the mercury, a vicious neurotoxin. I suggested to the anti-vaxxers that opposition to mercury, per se, could backfire, since it was not the only problem, and, depending on one’s perspective, might not have been the worst one. Here’s an example that pertains to the Yurko baby, from neurosurgeon Russell Blaylock, MD (who has also spoken out on Geoengineering/Chemtrails).

The bottom line is that removal of mercury from most vaccines (but not current multi-dose flu vaccines), gave the impression to many that voilà! vaccines were safe. What this effectively did was give activists another hill to climb, namely, the remaining issues, mainly side effects. Had the focus been on vaccines in general, mercury included, the campaign might have had a broader impact.

Before I move on to 5G – and the reader might already be getting the point – I refer to the foundational physiological and philosophical questions about immunity that struck me in the face as I studied basic anatomy and physiology in massage school.

Since I’ve already written about the problem with 5G (per se) opposition in my article on the wireless threat, I’ll just pull an excerpt from it:

…potential flaws exist in the growing opposition to 5G, including Senator Blumenthal’s (D-CT) request to the FCC for proof of safety. This request could amount to mere window dressing if the FCC is allowed to get away with the ruse used to declare 1G-to-4G safe.

Secondly, the safety/health backlash probably should not be aimed at 5G, but at wireless tech per se, 5G included. Otherwise, protesting 5G alone could lend an aura of acceptability to 4G, especially if 5G resistance has effect, and especially with the high level of 4G dependencies, addiction and obsession.
